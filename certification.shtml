Certification

Certification is offered by StormwaterEdu but can also be offered or co-sponsored by state, regional, or local government entities or private NGO’s who work with us to provide the course. Certification means the person earning the certificate has successfully completed the training recognized on the certificate and has passed an examination to certify competency in the subject matter. Certification is not a substitution for professional licensing or practice acts. Our certification courses may be used to provide PDH’s or continuing education credits.

Certification Goals

  1. Understand stormwater, how it affects water quality, and regulations associated with it.
  2. Understand stormwater management devices and how they function.
  3. Understand inspection and maintenance requirements of each stormwater practice.

The certification lasts for 3 years, after which we recommend a half day recertification course to learn about new developments in stormwater science.

Upon passing the certification exam, the certified individual will receive a certificate and inspector card.

Recertification

The science and practice of stormwater management continues to evolve. Update classes are available to renew certificates and to learn about the latest developments in stormwater science and regulation. This class will be 4-5 hours of lecture with no exam. Upon completion, receive an updated inspector card and certificate, valid for 3 years.

Course Offerings

The 1.5 day course spends the morning of day 1 giving background on watersheds, water quality, regulations, and the scientific and engineering basis of stormwater control measures. The remainder of the course focuses on operation and maintenance of specific stormwater control measures. The 1 day course starts with a short introduction to stormwater and stormwater control and goes directly to individual stormwater control measures.
